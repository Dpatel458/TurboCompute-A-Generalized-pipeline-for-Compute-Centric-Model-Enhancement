Gradient Landscape

Base Model

Mask/Prune Model

The dense network demonstrates a complex, high-curvature non-convex topology with numerous narrow minima and saddle points. Such sharp minima correspond to large Hessian eigenvalues, making gradient descent sensitive to learning rate selection and prone to oscillatory behavior.

Pruning imposes structural sparsity, effectively restricting optimization to a lower-dimensional subspace. This constraint smooths the curvature profile, reduces extreme Hessian eigenvalues, and promotes wider, flatter basins of attraction. Consequently, gradient descent trajectories become more stable, exhibit reduced oscillations, and converge more efficiently toward well-conditioned minima.

This transformation highlights how structural sparsification influences the geometry of the loss surface and the dynamics of optimization.
